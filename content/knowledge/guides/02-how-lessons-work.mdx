## TL;DR
Doting lessons are short by design: one focused prompt, one doable gesture, and one message/card output you can use immediately.[^1][^2]

## Why this matters
Connection often breaks down from friction, not lack of love: bad timing, low bandwidth, and unclear words.
A structured lesson loop lowers that friction.
Research supports maintenance behaviors, responsive communication, and regular rituals as meaningful contributors to relationship stability and satisfaction.[^1][^3][^4]
When people are physically apart, intentional digital communication can play a major role in felt closeness.[^5]

## Do this now
- [ ] Choose one person and one focus: appreciation, repair, or check-in.
- [ ] Run an AI-guided Prompt for that single focus.
- [ ] Pick one micro-gesture under 5 minutes.
- [ ] Draft one card message you can reuse later.
- [ ] Send now; do not wait to make it perfect.
- [ ] Schedule a short follow-up question for tomorrow.

## Your Doting Prompt
“Act as a relationship coach (non-clinical). I want to strengthen connection with <person>.
Relationship: <partner/family member>.
Context: <recent tension or distance>.
Constraints: <under 10 minutes, low energy, budget if any>.
Tone: <grounded and caring>.
Give me: (1) one 2-minute gesture, (2) one text draft, (3) one customizable card message, (4) one follow-up question that avoids blame.”

## One Small Gesture (doable today)
**Gesture:** “Single-topic check-in”  
- **Time:** 8–10 minutes  
- **Cost:** $0  
- **How:** Ask one question and only reflect back what you heard; skip advice.
- **Make it easier:** Use a three-line structure: “What I heard / what mattered / one next step.”

## Copy/paste scripts
**Text message**  
“I want to do better at staying connected, especially when we are both tired. Would a 10-minute check-in tonight help?”

**Repair attempt line**  
“I care about understanding you first. Can I try again and just listen?”

## If this feels awkward
- If you over-explain, shorten to one clear sentence.
- If conversations spiral, pause and return to one topic.
- If emotions run hot, switch to a brief card message and revisit later.
- If distance is the issue, increase predictability rather than message length.

## For couples
Use “first response wins”: when your partner shares something small, respond warmly before solving. That first response shapes the tone.[^3]

## For families
Use “ritualized touchpoints” (same day/time cue) so children, parents, and caregivers can count on connection despite changing schedules.[^4]

## When to get extra support
If someone feels unsafe, threatened, or in immediate danger, contact local emergency services.  
If you are in the U.S. and in crisis, call, text, or chat **988**: https://988lifeline.org/.[^6][^7]
