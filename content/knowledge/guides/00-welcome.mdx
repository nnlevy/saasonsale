## TL;DR
Start small and stay consistent: one thoughtful prompt, one doable gesture, and one short card can improve how connected people feel across busy schedules.[^1][^2]

## Why this matters
Most people do not struggle because they do not care. They struggle because real life is loud: different shifts, school logistics, caregiving, long commutes, and emotional fatigue.
When contact gets rushed, people miss bids for connection and assume the worst.
A repeatable structure helps: use AI-guided Prompts to reduce decision fatigue, do one small Gesture today, and save connection with a short card when time is tight.[^1][^3]
Small, repeated maintenance behaviors are associated with stronger relationship quality over time.[^1][^2]

## Do this now
- [ ] Open Doting and pick one person you want to feel closer to this week.
- [ ] Set a 2-minute timer and jot one thing they are carrying right now.
- [ ] Run one AI-guided Prompt (template below).
- [ ] Choose one Gesture you can complete today in under 10 minutes.
- [ ] Send one short customizable card message before the day ends.
- [ ] Add a reminder for your next check-in (same time tomorrow or this weekend).

## Your Doting Prompt
“Act as a relationship coach (non-clinical). I want to strengthen connection with <person>.
Relationship: <partner/co-parent/sibling/parent/child/caregiver>.
Context: <what has been hard lately>.
Constraints: <time/money/energy/time zone>.
Tone: <warm, calm, direct, light>.
Give me: (1) one 2-minute micro-gesture, (2) one text message draft, (3) one short card message I can customize, (4) one follow-up question for tomorrow.”

## One Small Gesture (doable today)
**Gesture:** “Two-minute appreciation ping”  
- **Time:** 2–4 minutes  
- **Cost:** $0  
- **How:** Send one specific appreciation: what they did + impact on you.  
- **Make it easier:** Use voice-to-text and keep it to one sentence.

## Copy/paste scripts
**Text message**  
“Quick note: I noticed how you handled today, and it made things easier for me. Thank you for showing up.”

**Card message (customizable, 63 words)**  
“I know life has been full lately, and I do not want that to hide what matters most: you matter to me. I appreciate the everyday things you do that often go unseen. I am trying to be more intentional, one small step at a time. Thank you for being in this with me. I am on your team.”

**Repair attempt line**  
“I got short earlier. I care about us more than being right—can we reset?”

## If this feels awkward
- If it feels scripted, say that out loud: “I’m practicing being more intentional.”
- If they respond briefly, do not force depth; send one warm follow-up tomorrow.
- If timing is bad, ask for a better time instead of pushing.
- If you missed a day, restart with a 30-second message. Consistency beats intensity.

## For couples
Use a “daily handoff” ritual: one message at the start of day, one at the end. Keep both under 60 seconds so it survives busy days.[^6]

## For families
Use a “bridge message” when apart (travel, co-parenting, caregiving): one practical update + one caring sentence so logistics do not replace warmth.[^5]

## When to get extra support
If someone feels unsafe, threatened, or in immediate danger, contact local emergency services.  
If you are in the U.S. and in crisis, call, text, or chat **988**: https://988lifeline.org/.[^3][^4]
