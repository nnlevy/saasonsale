## TL;DR
A bid is a small invitation (“look at this,” a sigh, a meme). Responding warmly and consistently is associated with stronger connection over time.[^1][^2]

## Why this matters
Bids are easy to miss when people are tired, multitasking, or in different time zones.
Miss enough bids, and both people may feel unseen.
Turning toward does not require a perfect response. It requires a timely, human one.
Research and clinical observation in relationship work highlight the value of responsive interactions and maintenance behaviors.[^1][^3]
For families apart, quick acknowledgment can protect closeness when logistics dominate conversations.[^4][^5]

## Do this now
- [ ] Pick one person whose bids you often miss.
- [ ] Decide one signal you will watch for today (sigh, link, “guess what”).
- [ ] Use a 2-line response: acknowledge + invite.
- [ ] Run the prompt below to generate a message and card variant.
- [ ] Send one response within 10 minutes of receiving a bid.

## Your Doting Prompt
“Act as a relationship coach (non-clinical). I want to strengthen connection with <person>.
Relationship: <partner/family member>.
Context: <we miss each other’s bids when busy>.
Constraints: <limited time, maybe different time zone, low energy>.
Tone: <warm and concise>.
Give me: (1) one 2-minute micro-gesture to turn toward, (2) one text reply draft, (3) one short customizable card message, (4) one follow-up question for later.”

## One Small Gesture (doable today)
**Gesture:** “10-minute bid window”  
- **Time:** 10 minutes total (spread across day)  
- **Cost:** $0  
- **How:** For one day, reply to bids within 10 minutes using acknowledgment + one question.  
- **Make it easier:** If immediate response is impossible, send: “I saw this—want to hear fully at <time>?”

## Copy/paste scripts
**Text message**  
“I saw your message. I care about this—what was the biggest part of it for you?”

**Voice-note outline**
- “I caught your bid and wanted to answer now.”
- “What I heard was…”
- “I’m curious about…”
- “I’m with you; tell me more when you can.”

## If this feels awkward
- If it feels forced, keep it brief and sincere.
- If you miss a bid, repair quickly: acknowledge the miss without excuses.
- If someone sends many bids at once, choose one and respond well.
- If conflict starts, switch to validation before problem-solving.

## For couples
Use bids during transitions (before work, after commute, before sleep). These moments are small but high-impact for tone-setting.[^1]

## For families
When children or relatives share “tiny” things, treat them as connection attempts, not interruptions. That pattern builds trust over time.[^2]

## When to get extra support
If someone feels unsafe, threatened, or in immediate danger, contact local emergency services.  
If you are in the U.S. and in crisis, call, text, or chat **988**: https://988lifeline.org/.[^6][^7]
