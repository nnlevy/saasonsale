## TL;DR
A good micro-lesson ends with action: one AI-guided Prompt, one Gesture, and one card-ready message you can actually send today.[^1][^2]

## Why this matters
People often wait for a “better time” to reconnect. That better time rarely appears.
Micro-lessons work because they lower the start-up cost: fewer decisions, clearer next steps, less emotional guesswork.
Research on relationship maintenance suggests frequent, practical behaviors matter more than occasional grand efforts.[^1][^2]
When people are apart—by distance, work, or custody schedules—small predictable touchpoints can reduce drift and misunderstanding.[^4][^5]

## Do this now
- [ ] Pick one lesson window this week (even 7 minutes).
- [ ] Decide your “minimum win”: one message sent.
- [ ] Run a prompt to generate a gesture and short message options.
- [ ] Choose the easiest option, not the most impressive one.
- [ ] Send a customizable card message or text immediately.
- [ ] Note what got in the way so tomorrow’s attempt is easier.

## Your Doting Prompt
“Act as a relationship coach (non-clinical). I want to strengthen connection with <person>.
Relationship: <partner/family member>.
Context: <what has felt distant lately>.
Constraints: <7 minutes max, low energy, maybe different time zones>.
Tone: <steady, kind, not overly sentimental>.
Give me: (1) one 2-minute gesture, (2) one text draft, (3) one short customizable card message, (4) one follow-up question for the next check-in.”

## One Small Gesture (doable today)
**Gesture:** “Calendar anchor”  
- **Time:** 5 minutes  
- **Cost:** $0  
- **How:** Put a recurring 7-minute connection block on your calendar and send one message during the first block.  
- **Make it easier:** If recurring blocks fail, attach it to an existing routine (after lunch, after school pickup, before lights out).

## Copy/paste scripts
**Voice-note outline**
- “Thinking of you and wanted one quick check-in.”
- “One thing I appreciated today was…”
- “One practical update: …”
- “Question for you: what felt heavy or good today?”

**Card message (56 words)**  
“Life is busy, but I do not want us to run only on logistics. I care about how you are, not just what got done. I am practicing tiny, consistent care instead of waiting for perfect moments. You matter to me, and I am glad we are in each other’s corner.”

## If this feels awkward
- If you skip lessons, shorten the goal to one line sent.
- If it feels robotic, personalize one phrase (“this reminded me of you because…”).
- If someone is unresponsive, stay respectful and lower frequency.
- If your first plan is too big, cut it in half.

## For couples
Choose one “state of us” question weekly: “What would make this week feel lighter for us?” Keep answers brief and practical.[^2]

## For families
When apart, pair emotion + logistics in one note: “I’m proud of you” + “I’ll call after dinner.” This keeps warmth and coordination together.[^5]

## When to get extra support
If someone feels unsafe, threatened, or in immediate danger, contact local emergency services.  
If you are in the U.S. and in crisis, call, text, or chat **988**: https://988lifeline.org/.[^3][^6]
